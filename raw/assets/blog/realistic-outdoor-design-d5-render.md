Lighting is a game changer when it comes to outdoor design, and [D5 Render](https://www.d5render.com/downloading?utm_source=seo&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=realistic-outdoor-design-d5-render) makes it effortless. You can choose from three options:

- ‍[Geo & Sky](https://www.d5render.com/posts/geo-sky-in-d5-render) lets you quickly adjust the sun's position and time of day.

## 5. Frame Your Outdoor Design Like a Photographer

Great outdoor design isn't just about what's in the scene—it's also how it's framed. With [D5 Render](https://www.d5render.com/downloading?utm_source=seo&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=realistic-outdoor-design-d5-render), you can [set up your camera](https://www.d5render.com/posts/camera-setting-tips-must-known-for-archviz) just like a real-world shoot. Start by choosing a vertical or cinematic aspect ratio that matches your reference image. Then enable two‑point perspective to keep verticals clean and avoid that "leaning wall" effect.

Next, pick a focal length that suits your story: go shorter for a wide, immersive view or longer to compress space and focus on key elements.

Once you've locked your camera—don't keep nudging it. Stay disciplined and build your outdoor design around one strong composition as you would for a photo shoot.

With [D5 Render](https://www.d5render.com/downloading?utm_source=seo&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=realistic-outdoor-design-d5-render)'s real‑time camera tools, you can explore angles, fine‑tune framing, and instantly preview the result. Your final render won't just look like CGI—it will feel like a captured moment in real space.
