# How to Paint Realistic Grass in 3 Steps

[Embedded video](https://www.youtube.com/embed/dg9qwlNx7lQ)

You've modeled the perfect architecture and set the ideal lighting, but your scene falls flat the moment you add grass—it looks like a plastic carpet, destroys your render times, or never quite achieves that wild, natural look.

This tutorial is your way out. We'll show you a proven three-step workflow in [D5 Render](https://www.d5render.com/downloading?utm_source=seo&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=how-to-render-photorealistic-grass-in-3-steps) that transforms this common bottleneck into one of your greatest assets. Learn to master the key tools that give you control over variety, placement, and density, turning barren ground into photorealistic meadows in no time.

## Key Takeaways on Paint Realistic Grass in D5 Render

- Set a realistic base: apply a woodland/lawn material to the terrain in [D5 Render](https://www.d5render.com/downloading?utm_source=seo&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=how-to-render-photorealistic-grass-in-3-steps) and scale the UVs to create a dense ground texture for photorealistic grass.
- Build natural variety fast: use the Scatter tool to paint multiple grass models at different heights/greens, erase random patches, and add fallen leaves to break uniformity.
- Add layered depth for realism: line edges with shrubs, mix in mid-ground plants, and place a tall foreground tree—then leverage the D5 Asset Library for quick, real-time landscape visualization.

## Step 01: Set a Lawn Material

First, choose a woodland material and apply it onto the model surface.

![Section drawing of realistic grass painted in 3 steps with D5 Render](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/62ce5d829e01c60b7c148396/6512b04224595224b1125817_Snipaste_2023-09-26_17-35-24.jpg)

Stretch its UV to get a dense look.

![Section drawing of realistic grass painted in 3 steps with D5 Render](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/62ce5d829e01c60b7c148396/68ff1e646080c2267460010a_6512b04ca94fee6449344661_UV-ezgif.com-resize.gif)

## Step 02: Paint Grass Models

Then, choose a grass model to fill the lawn with the 'Scatter' tool.

Repeat the step on another kind of higher, greener grass.

![Section drawing of realistic grass painted in 3 steps with D5 Render](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/62ce5d829e01c60b7c148396/68ff21a69715505f6f953b31_6512b08992b0b85de8621e3f_-ezgif.com-resize.gif)

Remove a bit of the lawn randomly with the Eraser tool.

![Section drawing of realistic grass painted in 3 steps with D5 Render](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/62ce5d829e01c60b7c148396/68ff2740c37dd14185b043e0_6512b0931da5ba39670ebe09_-ezgif.com-resize.gif)

You can also add some scattered fallen leaves.

## Step 03: Add Decorative Plants

Place some shrubs along the edge.

And intersperse some in the middle.

A tall tree at the foreground will make the scene look more layered.

![Section drawing of realistic grass painted in 3 steps with D5 Render](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/62ce5d829e01c60b7c148396/68ff28fd3839ff5e1cabd872_6512b0c37e4508fa00f939ed_-ezgif.com-optimize.gif)
