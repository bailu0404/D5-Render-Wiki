# D5 February Recap | Structured Adoption, Real-Time from Day One

February marked a turning point.

D5 Lite lowered the barrier to entry. AI removed technical friction. D5 3.0 delivered production-grade power. And across studios, real-time visualization shifted from experimentation to implementation.

From governance in AEC visualization to AI-driven real-time design, here’s everything that moved the conversation forward this month.

Designed specifically for early-stage design in SketchUp, D5 Lite brings AI-powered, real-time visualization directly into the modeling process. No exporting. No switching tools. No waiting for final renders before evaluating ideas.

February reinforced a clear direction: real-time visualization is becoming operational, not experimental.

With structured adoption discussions, AI-assisted efficiency, and scalable production workflows, firms are integrating D5 more deliberately into their design and delivery processes.

The focus is no longer on features alone, but on workflow reliability, team alignment, and measurable efficiency gains.

That transition is now in motion.
