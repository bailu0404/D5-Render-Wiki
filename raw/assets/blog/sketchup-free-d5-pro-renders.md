- ‍****Step 1: Model a small residential or interior scene in SketchUp Free.****Begin by using SketchUp Free's intuitive, web-based tools to construct the basic geometry and structure of your design, whether it's the layout of a room or the form of a small building. Focus on getting the core shapes and dimensions right, as this will be the foundation for your rendering.****‍****
- ****Step 2: Export the .skp file.****Once your initial modeling is complete in SketchUp Free, simply export your work as an .skp file. This standard SketchUp file format is easily generated from the web interface and serves as the direct bridge to bring your model into D5 Render.****‍****
- ****Step 3: Import into D5 Render and apply materials, lighting, and camera setup.****With your .skp file ready, open [D5 Render](https://www.d5render.com/downloading?utm_source=seo&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=sketchup-free) and import the model; it will appear in the D5 workspace, ready for its transformation. Now, unleash D5's power by applying realistic PBR materials from the extensive built-in library, setting up dynamic lighting to create mood and depth, and positioning cameras to capture the most compelling views of your design.
