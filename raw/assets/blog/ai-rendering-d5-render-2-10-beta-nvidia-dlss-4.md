## ****Understanding NVIDIA DLSS and Its Evolution****

Alongside the launch of GeForce RTX 50 Series GPUs, NVIDIA debuted DLSS 4 at CES with Multi Frame Generation to boost frame rates by using AI to generate up to three frames per rendered frame. It works in unison with the suite of DLSS technologies to increase performance by up to 8x over traditional rendering. With it, designers can enjoy ultra-smooth visuals with frame rates increased by up to four times.

DLSS 4 also introduces the graphics industry’s first real-time application of the transformer model architecture. Transformer-based DLSS Ray Reconstruction and Super Resolution models use 2x more parameters and 4x more compute to provide greater stability. By replacing traditional denoisers with an AI-powered network, DLSS 4 generates higher-quality pixels between sampled rays. This significantly reduces issues like flickering noise, motion blur, and missing details, especially in scenes with complex lighting and ray tracing.
