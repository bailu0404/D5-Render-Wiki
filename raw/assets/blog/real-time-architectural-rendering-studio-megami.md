When Brazilian architect and visualizer ****Lucas Castro**** first saw [D5 Render](https://www.d5render.com/downloading?utm_source=seo&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=studio-megami) in a YouTube trailer, he didn’t quite believe what he was seeing—global illumination, real-time ray tracing, and cinematic lighting all happening live on screen.

At the time, he had spent years teaching Lumion and delivering visualizations the traditional way: waiting hours for each render, balancing hardware limits, and paying a steep annual license fee.

Then came one pivotal week, "I took a leap of faith. I bought it—and it paid for itself in two months."

That single decision changed his entire business.

Today, his studio runs faster, delivers more, earns more—and inspires the next generation of visualizers to do the same.

## ****Studio Megami: Visualization as a Language****

Founded in 2018, [****Studio Megami****](https://www.studiomegami.com/) operates from Brazil and serves architects, developers, and construction firms across the globe.

Roughly half of Lucas’s clients are based in Brazil, with the rest spread between ****Europe (Netherlands, Portugal)**** and the ****United States****, including a long-time architectural partner in California.

![real-time architectural rendering](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/62ce5d829e01c60b7c148396/69081faa618963630d171f1f_hard%20rock%20hotel.gif)

The team stays lean—****two to three artists****, occasionally scaling to five for large-scale projects like ****Hard Rock Hotel Gramado****.  
For Lucas, visualization isn’t an add-on to architecture; it’s the bridge that turns ideas into belief.

> **Some clients can’t imagine a space from a floor plan. A good render is what makes them** ******feel****** **the design.**

## ****Why D5 Became the Core of His Workflow****

Before D5, Lucas relied on Lumion. It worked—but it was expensive and demanding.

> **Lumion was so heavy that my computer couldn’t handle large projects. I had to split scenes, render small portions, and spend hours waiting.**

Then came D5.

The same scenes that once took ****hours to render**** now take ****five minutes at 4K resolution****. The real-time lighting engine made testing instant. And D5’s performance made even massive, vegetation-rich environments possible on his setup.

That speed didn’t just make work easier—it made new kinds of projects achievable.

With D5, Lucas began taking on projects that had once been out of reach — larger in scale, richer in atmosphere, and more cinematic in scope. Beyond traditional still images, he started producing videos, event visualizations, and expansive outdoor scenes.

## Hero Project: A House in the Mountains of Evergreen, Colorado

Among Lucas’s portfolio, one project stands out: a ****mountain residence in Evergreen, Colorado, United States****, designed by a Colorado based studio, [****Sexton Studio****](https://www.instagram.com/sexton.studio/).

Lucas was commissioned by [****Raydon Concepts****](https://www.instagram.com/raydonconcepts/), led by ****Brandon Ray Henderson****, a long-time collaborator and D5 enthusiast. Together, they have developed a smooth transcontinental workflow—combining Raydon’s creative direction and Lucas’s visualization expertise in D5 Render.

The client, a California-based architect, provided only the SketchUp model. Everything else—the atmosphere, lighting, materials, and storytelling—was up to Lucas.

The Evergreen project challenged everything about large-scale, real-time visualization.

![real-time architectural rendering](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/62ce5d829e01c60b7c148396/690820a7c41ff49b4edb5f1c_studio%20megami%202.png)

Set in the Rockies, the site spans ****seven blocks of mountainous terrain****, covered in thousands of trees, layered rock formations, and native vegetation that demanded careful realism.

> **It was one of the heaviest scenes I’ve ever built. Even though D5 is optimized, the vegetation density was insane.**

Each 4K image rendered in under ****five minutes****, transforming what would once have been an overnight job into a same-day delivery.

![real-time architectural rendering](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/62ce5d829e01c60b7c148396/690820c7561e7a164cfbb3e3_studio%20megami%203.jpg)

When Lucas delivered the visuals, ****Sexton Studio**** immediately shared them online—capturing the serene, tactile spirit of mountain living with near-photographic quality.

He’s now preparing another project for the same client using D5—five times larger.

### ****Designing Emotion Through Light****

![real-time architectural rendering](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/62ce5d829e01c60b7c148396/690820eb618963630d172fba_studio%20megami%204.jpg)

For Lucas, rendering goes beyond geometry and texture—it’s about emotion. “If you know what emotion you want to express, you can set the light to match it. Everything else—materials, colors, details—comes after.”

Every scene begins not with objects, but with light.  
He shapes atmosphere before materials, using illumination to breathe life into space—to make viewers **feel** the story before it’s even built.

The secret behind many of his most striking images is ****D5’s natural lighting system****.

> **The way the sun hits the leaves in D5—how they glow or darken—is so precise.** [**That’s what makes the scene** ******feel****** **alive.**](https://www.d5render.com/downloading?utm_source=seo&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=studio-megami)

![real-time architectural rendering](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/62ce5d829e01c60b7c148396/69082112ae85bacc5332ab36_studio%20megami%205.jpg)

It’s a simple belief that has become the foundation of both his art and his teaching.

## ****Teaching the Next Generation of Visualizers****

Beyond running Studio Megami, Lucas teaches 3D visualization to students and small firms. He once taught Lumion and V-Ray—but now, he starts every class with D5.

> **When a student asks to learn V-Ray, I say: try D5 first. It’s easier, faster, cheaper—and the quality is on par.**

He calls learing D5 **“like playing a video game”**—intuitive, responsive, and rewarding even for beginners. Within ****two days****, his students can produce client-ready renders.

For Lucas, this accessibility matters. It allows new designers to ****focus on creativity, not settings****, and helps small studios become profitable without large teams or render farms.

He’s now developing a ****new online course**** centered on D5’s ****AI tools****—AI Make Seamless, AI PBR Material Snap, and AI Agent—to show how technology can empower artistry rather than replace it.

## ****Speed That Redefines What’s Possible****

The power of D5 isn’t just in realism—it’s in reliability under pressure.

Lucas recalls an urgent request from an event-planning client who needed ****10 images within two days****. "She sent the 3D model at night. I finished everything in six hours—and even made a short video as a gift.”

A job that once took 48 hours was done in one-eighth of the time. For Lucas, that kind of agility translates directly into business growth.

- ‍****More projects per month**** thanks to faster turnaround.
- ‍****Higher pricing power**** justified by speed and quality.
- ‍****Repeat clients**** who trust he can deliver under pressure.

> **D5 gives me the courage to promise faster delivery—because I know I can do it.**

### ****Delivering Clarity—and Fewer Revisions****

Real-time visualization also transformed how Lucas collaborates with clients.  
Instead of rendering multiple drafts, he sends ****screenshots straight from D5’s viewport**** for quick approval.

> **Sometimes they approve on the first try. What they see in D5 previews already looks like the final image.**

That immediacy shortens review cycles, reduces misunderstandings, and builds trust—especially with international clients working across time zones.
