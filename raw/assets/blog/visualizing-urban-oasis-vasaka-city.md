[****Vasaka City****](https://vasakacity.co.id/) is anything but modest. Sprawling over 650 hectares in Bekasi, Indonesia, this envisioned smart township fuses residential clusters, retail hubs, healthcare centers, and green recreational spaces into one cohesive masterplan. With six architectural firms across Indonesia and Hong Kong contributing, the question loomed: How do you narrate the story of an entire city before a single brick is laid?

For ****Yoga4Arch Studio****, a lean team based in Lampung, this assignment transcended typical rendering—it was a chance to shape investor perceptions, stakeholder insight, and future residents' first impressions. The key to meeting this challenge? D5 Render.

## ****About the Studio: Yoga4Arch****

Founded in 2011 by architect-visualizer ****Yoga Binhar****, [Yoga4Arch](https://www.behance.net/YOGA4ARCH) began as a local residential design studio handling layouts, site planning, and documentation. As demand grew, so did their capabilities—Over time, the team expanded into 3D visualization to deliver a more complete architectural package.

Originally serving the Lampung region, a consistent presence on YouTube and Facebook helped them expand. Today, Yoga4Arch serves clients across Indonesia and has started attracting international projects.

## ****Project Overview: the Urban Oasis - Vasaka City****

Vasaka City is an ambitious smart city initiative by [Waskita Modernland](https://www.modernland.co.id/projects/view/vasaka-city). The masterplan includes a variety of programmatic zones—from residential neighborhoods and retail corridors to hospitals and high-rise towers. With six different architectural teams contributing to the vision, coordination and visual cohesion were key.

****Yoga4Arch Studio was tasked with producing:****

- High-quality aerial views and bird's-eye renders
- Cinematic animations for marketing
- Enhanced 3D details like roof tiles, vegetation, and environmental context
- Mood and atmosphere setting to communicate the "Urban Oasis" concept

But scale brought challenges. Visualizing 650 hectares meant processing enormous models, handling detailed environments, and rendering complex lighting scenarios—fast. That’s where the cracks in their old workflow began to show.

## ****From Limitation to Liberation: Why D5 Was the Right Fit****

As Yoga4Arch scaled up its visualization work for Vasaka City, their existing software toolchain hit a wall. They had relied on [Lumion](https://www.d5render.com/posts/d5-render-lumion-architectural-design-rendering?utm_source=seo&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=yoga4arch) for years, but the massive site plan for Vasaka—spanning over 650 hectares—exceeded its limits. Even with a capable machine, Lumion failed to load models reliably once the scene crossed the 300-hectare mark.

## ****Behind the Scenes: Solving Creative Chaos****

Working with six different architects—across countries, time zones, and aesthetic tastes—isn’t for the faint of heart. Often, Yoga and his team played the role of translator, balancing bold ideas with buildable outcomes.

Revisions? Plenty. But the visual clarity D5 offered allowed smoother approvals and faster alignment. Scenes were adjusted in real time, then sent as screenshots before final renders—keeping communication tight and expectations aligned.

## ****The Workflow: Breaking Down a Giant****

To make such a massive project manageable, the team didn’t try to conquer the entire site at once. Instead, they divided Vasaka City into ~22 blocks, rendering and refining each section individually.

- ‍****Geo-Based Storytelling****   
  Using Google Earth as a base, they recreated the actual terrain of Bekasi—rice paddies, roads, even rural homes on the edges. It wasn’t fantasy; it was familiar.

- ‍****Real-Time Sky and Lighting  
  ‍****D5’s geosky lighting control lets them fine-tune sunlight across hours, capturing golden-hour glow and casting real-time cloud shadows over rooftops.

![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/62ce5d829e01c60b7c148396/68627999b8b0a50b4ed2d735_5eecdaf48460cde5261a82cee90f4dc4f7454550510369a075b8339e1c4c2483f728f271dbcdaa6639e8703ac5556d0d311f0bd38b2a38ea760d004d7c5b108750bd79eeb9ced1fcb301aa4a2d2a0af9fb620853026584f3c49ca25a33863d3e.gif)

- ‍****Manual Exposure for Maximum Mood   
  ‍****Instead of using auto-exposure (which often blows out subtle skies), they dialed it in manually—ensuring every scene had the right emotional tone.****‍****

- ****Vegetation at Scale  
  ‍****One of D5’s unsung powers: handling [dense, large-scale vegetation with ease](https://www.d5render.com/post/professional-landscape-architecture-software-d5-render?utm_source=seo&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=yoga4arch). Not cloned greens, but believable biodiversity.

All this came together in just minutes per render—and sometimes, the team even skipped Photoshop touch-ups. “What you see,” Yoga explained, “is exactly what we’d build.”

And it all happened in just 8 weeks. “A project like this can feel like a marathon,” Yoga admitted, “but with D5, it was one we could actually finish.”
