## ****A Vision Brought to Life****

[Reta Proje](https://retaproje.com/?utm_source=d5blog&utm_medium=seo&utm_campaign=reta) is no ordinary architectural firm. Founded in 1993 and headquartered in Ankara, this multidisciplinary powerhouse has made waves in architecture, landscape, and urban design, serving clients across Turkey, the Middle East, and Europe. Innovation has always been at the heart of their approach—so much so that their founder was rendering in 3D back in 1997 when most of the industry was still working in 2D.

However, as demand for high-quality, immersive design presentations skyrocketed, the firm found itself at a crossroads. Slow rendering times, software limitations, and an increasingly competitive market meant they needed to evolve—or risk falling behind.

When Reta Proje discovered ****D5 Render****, it was more than just an upgrade—it was a paradigm shift. The firm embraced real-time rendering, allowing their team to bring designs to life with unprecedented speed and fluidity. No longer constrained by lengthy rendering processes, they found new creative freedom, iterating ideas instantly and refining details on the go. The result? A transformation that not only elevated efficiency but also redefined what was possible in architectural visualization.

## ****Challenges Before Using D5 Render****

### ****When Rendering Slows Down Creativity****

Before integrating D5 Render, Reta Proje relied on ****3ds Max, V-Ray, and Lumion**** for their rendering needs. While these tools had long been industry staples, they came with a major drawback: speed. Rendering large-scale projects could take hours, sometimes overnight, creating frustrating bottlenecks in the creative process.

“The old way slowed us down. We'd make a change, render, wait, review, and repeat. It was exhausting,” recalls ****Tanya Akdag, Architect at Reta Proje****. “We needed something faster, something intuitive.”

### ****The Struggle of Real-Time Collaboration****

With a team working across ****SketchUp, Rhino, and Revit****, seamless collaboration was crucial. Yet, their existing tools weren't built for dynamic teamwork. Any small tweak in the design required lengthy updates, which made it difficult to respond quickly to client feedback. As the industry became increasingly fast-paced, Reta Proje needed a way to ****iterate designs in real-time**** without losing momentum.
