## What is Ambient Occlusion(AO)?

Ambient Occlusion (AO) is a terminology in computational graphics. Generally speaking, it refers to technology that enables the renderer to present a more realistic light effect for your work by simulating indirect lighting and diffuse reflections in real life.

D5 Render enables Ambient Occlusion in both ****preview and rendering****.

You can switch on "AO" in the effect panel and adjust its Radius to control the extent to which a location on the surface is obscured from surrounding light sources.

## How to use Ambient Occlusion?

### 1. Use the ****AO map**** to enhance the details of light and shadow

AO maps help enhance the shadows in a scene. Visualizers usually use the AO map in Photoshop to produce a more realistic light-and-shadow contrast.

To output an AO map in D5 Render, go to Image Render > Channels and check "AO".

![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/62ce5d829e01c60b7c148396/6891eb560ad3ebb68fe6a410_fbetr.png)

### 2. Use the ****AO rendering mode**** as a VFX

With D5 Render, AO can be a way of stylizing your work as well. You can achieve a special visual effect by turning on ****the "AO" mode****. If combined with ****"Outline" mode****, the rendering effect would be stunning and impressive.
